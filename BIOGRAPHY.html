Jhayco: The Sound of the New Wave

If you’re into Latin music, you already know Jhayco (formerly Jhay Cortez) isn’t just part of the movement—he’s leading it. Born Jesús Manuel Nieves Cortez on April 9, 1993, in Río Piedras, Puerto Rico, this guy went from writing bangers for the biggest names in reggaetón to becoming one of its top players.
stepping into the spotlight, Jhayco was behind the scenes crafting hits for Zion & Lennox, Daddy Yankee, and Ozuna. But once he dropped his own music? It was game over. With tracks like No Me Conoce (Remix), Dakiti, and Fiel, he built a sound that’s impossible to ignore.
What makes Jhayco different? He’s not here to follow trends—he’s making his own lane. Mixing reggaetón, trap, and electronic vibes, his music is always fresh, unpredictable, and straight-up addictive. His latest album, Vida Rockstar, proves that he’s only getting started.
From underground clubs to sold-out arenas, Jhayco’s wave keeps getting bigger. If you’re here, you already know—this is his era, and we’re all just vibing with it.
